Coffee - Life - Style

By 邱致瑋  -  June 03, 2023

Light roast coffee: A type of coffee that is roasted using a mild process. During the light roasting process, the coffee beans are roasted at lower temperatures, allowing them to retain more of their natural flavors and acidity. This roasting method brings out the unique fruity aroma and bright taste of the coffee beans. Light roast coffee has a lighter color with a slightly shiny surface. When tasting light roast coffee, you will notice its refreshing fruity or floral aroma accompanied by a mild acidity. It has a light-bodied and crisp sensation, often featuring a bright acidity, making it a popular choice, especially for those who prefer a lighter coffee flavor. Light roast coffee beans are commonly used in pour-over or drip brewing methods as they better showcase the bean's flavor characteristics. Whether you are a coffee novice or a coffee enthusiast, light roast coffee is worth a try to experience its flavor complexity and refreshing qualities that set it apart from dark roast coffees.

Medium roast coffee: A popular choice among coffee lovers. It is roasted for a moderate duration, striking a balance between the light and dark roast profiles. Medium roast coffee beans have a medium-brown color and a smoother surface. With medium roast coffee, you can expect a well-rounded flavor profile. It retains some of the acidity and brightness of light roast coffee while developing deeper caramelized notes. The aroma is often described as balanced, with hints of chocolate and nuttiness. Medium roast coffee offers a pleasant combination of acidity, body, and flavor complexity. It is versatile and suitable for various brewing methods, including drip brewing, French press, and espresso. Whether you prefer a smooth cup of black coffee or enjoy adding milk and sugar, medium roast coffee provides a delightful experience with its balanced flavors and versatility.

Dark roast coffee: Known as gentleman's roast, is a rich and bold coffee option. It is roasted for a longer duration, resulting in dark-brown to almost black beans with a shiny surface, often exhibiting oiliness. Dark roast coffee offers a robust and intense flavor profile. The longer roasting process reduces the acidity, producing a smoother and fuller-bodied cup. It showcases deep caramelized and chocolaty notes, sometimes accompanied by hints of smokiness. This type of coffee is commonly favored by those who enjoy a strong and bold flavor experience. It is well-suited for espresso-based drinks, as well as for individuals who prefer adding milk or cream to their coffee to complement its rich and robust taste. Dark roast coffee provides a satisfying and indulgent coffee experience for those seeking a full-bodied and intense flavor profile.
